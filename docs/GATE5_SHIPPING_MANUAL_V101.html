PART VII: GATE5 SHIPPING FRAMEWORK v100

Overview: The 60/90 Split Delivery System

GATE5 v100 STRATEGIC MODEL: Split delivery system optimized for landlord relationship management and PARTNER_TECHNICIAN workload reduction. Two separate delivery streams with 30-day offset ensure never more than 6 boxes per delivery (except 2 collision days per year).

Delivery Philosophy: Separate consumable ingredients (60-day fast cycle) from durable goods (90-day slow cycle). Complete ingredient sets ship together maintaining operational integrity while keeping delivery footprint manageable for building relationships.

3. LIQUID FORTRESS 2.0 PACKING PROTOCOL (BOX A)

Objective: Zero liquid escape. Each jug contained independently so single jug failure does not compromise entire shipment.

1 SEAL LID: Apply induction/heat seal to bottle lid using sealing machine. Verify seal integrity visually.
2 TIP TEST: Tip bottle 45° for 3 seconds. Check for drips at seal interface. If drip detected → reject jug, do not pack.
3 BAG JUG: Place jug into individual 6mm heavy-duty poly bag. Bag must be clean, no punctures.
4 SEAL BAG: Seal poly bag completely using heat sealer. No gaps, no openings. Bag acts as secondary containment.
5 PACK INTO BOX: Place bagged jug into BOX A with bottom corrugated liner (16×12) already in place. Do not nest jugs tightly—allow slight spacing.
6 REPEAT: Repeat steps 1-5 for each jug. Check cumulative weight after each jug added. Stop at 40 lbs (testing phase) or when reaching 4 jugs, whichever comes first.
7 ADD VOID-CAP: Place 4" corrugated void-cap on top of jugs for uniform compression during shipping.
8 FINAL WEIGH: Verify final weight ≤ 40 lbs before sealing box. Record weight in QC log (Gate 3).
CAP RULE: Solid Shipping Caps ONLY (Safety Red). Service Caps (White with pump spout) are a "Hard Fail" during transit. White caps may leak under pressure changes. Always verify red caps before packing.

7. DELIVERY EXECUTION PROTOCOLS

Driver Instructions: 60-Day Ingredient Delivery

1 SCAN & LOAD: At Hub, scan all 6 boxes (A, B1, B2, B3/C2, C1/C3, D). Verify scan confirmation in app before loading vehicle.
2 LOAD SEQUENCE: Load heaviest first (BOX A, B1), lightest on top (BOX D). Prevents crushing during transit.
3 ARRIVE & GPS CHECK: App validates within 50 meters of site. Green checkmark = proceed. Yellow warning = override possible but logged as LOCATION_ANOMALY.
4 UNLOAD & ARRANGE: Bring all 6 boxes inside. Arrange for POD photo—all boxes visible, color labels facing camera.
5 POD PHOTO: Take Proof of Delivery photo. App enforces: ≥1MB file size, brightness ≥30 luma, blur check passes, GPS within 50m, all 6 boxes visible.
6 SUBMIT POD: Tap "Submit POD" in app. No weight entry (Zero-Weight Rule). Timestamp auto-embedded.
7 PARTNER NOTIFICATION: App sends push notification to PARTNER_TECHNICIAN. 24-hour acceptance window starts immediately.

Driver Instructions: 90-Day Cup Delivery

1 PICKUP: Collect 4 factory-sealed cup boxes directly from supplier staging area. No Hub stop required.
2 SCAN: Scan CARTON E barcode (single scan covers all 4 boxes). Verify in app.
3 DELIVER: Standard delivery protocol (steps 3-7 above). POD photo must show all 4 factory boxes.
Cup Delivery Efficiency: Average cup delivery time: 8 minutes (vs. 12 minutes for ingredient delivery). Lighter weight (13 lbs vs. 200 lbs), fewer boxes to arrange, simpler POD capture. Cup deliveries are fastest in system.

PARTNER_TECHNICIAN Instructions: Accepting Deliveries

24-Hour Rule: Must accept or refuse delivery within 24 hours of POD timestamp. Failure to act triggers URGENT_ALERT to OPS_MANAGER.

Acceptance Sequence (Ingredient Delivery):

  1. Open app, navigate to "Pending Deliveries"
  2. Review POD photo—verify 6 boxes present, no visible damage
  3. Physically inspect boxes—check for crushing, wet spots, open seals
  4. If all boxes acceptable → Tap "Accept Delivery"
  5. If any box damaged → Tap "Refuse Delivery", select damaged box(es), take damage photos
  6. Begin refill process (transfer bags from boxes to bins per zone labels)

Acceptance Sequence (Cup Delivery):

  1. Review POD photo—verify 4 factory boxes present
  2. Physically inspect—factory seals intact, no crushing
  3. Accept in app
  4. Stack 4 boxes in 2×2 arrangement on floor within BLUE tape boundary
  5. Position: Next to machine (NOT under rack - insufficient clearance)
  6. Stack configuration: 2 boxes on floor (bottom row), 2 boxes stacked on top (top row)
  7. Verify stability - stack should be ~36" tall and occupy ~2 ft × 2 ft floor space
  8. No bin transfer required (cups stay in factory boxes until used)
Cup Placement Critical: Do NOT attempt to store cup boxes under rack. Bottom shelf clearance (~18-20") is insufficient for 36" cup stack. Boxes must remain on open floor next to machine within BLUE zone boundary. This is by design and documented in wayfinding labels.

Handling Collision Days: Two Options

Option A: Single 10-Box Delivery (RECOMMENDED)

  • Driver loads all 10 boxes (6 ingredient + 4 cup) in single trip
  • One POD photo showing all 10 boxes
  • PARTNER_TECHNICIAN accepts full shipment once
  • Refill sequence: Ingredients first (~90 min), cups second (~20 min)
  • No additional delivery cost

Pros: Simplest execution, one driver visit, one acceptance process
Cons: 10 boxes on 2 days per year (defeats "max 6 boxes" goal 20% of cup deliveries)

Option B: Split Same-Day Delivery

  • Morning delivery: 6 ingredient boxes
  • Afternoon delivery: 4 cup boxes (same driver, different route stop)
  • Two separate POD photos
  • PARTNER_TECHNICIAN accepts twice in one day
  • Adds $25/site × 2 collisions = $50/site/year additional cost

Pros: Maintains "max 6 boxes" principle 100% of time
Cons: Doubles delivery cost on collision days, more complex routing

BOARD APPROVED PROTOCOL: Use Option A (single 10-box delivery) on collision days. Twice-per-year occurrence of 10 boxes is acceptable trade-off versus doubling delivery costs. Still significantly better than original 9-box-every-delivery model.

10. QUALITY GATES FOR INGREDIENT SHIPMENTS

5-Gate QC applies to 6 ingredient boxes only. Cup shipments (CARTON E) bypass all gates—factory sealed, direct pickup.

The 5-Gate Sequence

GATE 1: Zero Check. Digital scale tared with 10 lb weight at shift start. Log calibration timestamp. Scale must read 10.00 lbs ±0.05 lbs to pass. Recalibrate if drift detected.
GATE 2: Freshness Lock. BOX B1 only: Verify every Oat/Matcha mix bag contains exactly one Oxygen Absorber 500cc before sealing box. No absorber = reject bag. Log absorber count.
GATE 3: Weight Law. Weigh each box after packing. Record weight in QC log. Standard: 20-40 lbs (no scale lock, logging only). If >40 lbs, ops manager reviews and decides: split box or accept overage with documentation.
GATE 4: Movement Test. Shake test: Lift box 6" off ground, shake gently. Listen for rattles. If rattle detected, add corrugated void-caps until zero-rattle achieved. Compression test: Press lid closed without forcing—should close smoothly.
GATE 5: Label Gate. Apply color-coded BOX ID label (4"×6" minimum) on SIDE of carton (not top). Must match zone color. Label must not cover carrier shipping label. Insert label into clear packing list envelope. Verify box ID matches manifest.

Quality Metrics & Rejection Thresholds

Gate Pass Rate Target Rejection Trigger
Gate 1 (Calibration) 100% Scale drift >0.1 lbs → Recalibrate immediately
Gate 2 (O₂ Absorbers) 100% Missing absorber → Reject bag, add new bag with absorber
Gate 3 (Weight) ≥95% >40 lbs → Ops manager review required
Gate 4 (Movement) ≥98% Rattle persists after void-cap → Repack box
Gate 5 (Label) 100% Wrong color label → Replace with correct zone color

12. EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS & CONTINGENCIES

Delivery Failure Scenarios

Scenario A: PARTNER Refuses Ingredient Delivery (Damage Detected)

  1. PARTNER documents damage with in-app photos (3 angles minimum)
  2. App notifies OPS_MANAGER immediately with photos
  3. Ops manager evaluates: minor (repack 1-2 boxes) vs. major (full reshpiment)
  4. If minor: Expedited repack and same-day redelivery authorized
  5. If major: Emergency 24-hour full reshipment triggered
  6. Site operates on existing inventory during emergency window

Scenario B: Driver Cannot Deliver (Site Access Denied)

  1. Driver documents access issue with photo + GPS timestamp
  2. Driver returns boxes to Hub (do not leave unattended)
  3. OPS_MANAGER contacts site within 2 hours to resolve access
  4. Redelivery scheduled within 48 hours
  5. If site repeatedly denies access (3× in 6 months) → Contract review

Scenario C: Collision Day Overload (10 Boxes Too Much for Site)

  1. Site contacts OPS_MANAGER before delivery day (min 48hr notice)
  2. Ops manager offers split delivery: Ingredients AM, Cups PM (+$25 fee)
  3. Or defer cup delivery by 7 days (moves to Day 127 instead of Day 120)
  4. Document preference in site notes for future collision days

Inventory Shortage Emergency

Trigger: Site reports <10% remaining on any ingredient before next scheduled delivery

Emergency Resupply Protocol:

  1. PARTNER reports shortage via app "Emergency Resupply" button
  2. App requires photo of nearly-empty bin as evidence
  3. OPS_MANAGER reviews consumption data: Actual emergency vs. misreporting?
  4. If validated: 2-day expedited shipment of shortage item only (not full delivery)
  5. Cost: $75 expedite fee (absorbed by SIPJOLT if usage spike confirmed)
  6. Ops reviews consumption pattern: Is 60-day cycle adequate for this site?

13. SUMMARY: KEY PRINCIPLES OF GATE5 v100

STRATEGIC PILLARS OF THE 60/90 SPLIT MODEL:
  1. Landlord Relationship Optimization: Never more than 6 boxes per typical delivery (80% of time) maintains professional commercial appearance
  2. Complete Ingredient Sets: All 60-day consumables ship together (BOX A-D) ensuring operational continuity without partial deliveries
  3. Product-Appropriate Cycles: Consumables (60-day fast cycle) logically separated from durable goods (90-day slow cycle)
  4. PARTNER Workload Management: Separate ingredient (~90 min) and cup (~20 min) refills reduce single-session fatigue and error rates
  5. Hub Efficiency: Cup shipments bypass 5-Gate QC (factory sealed) saving 8,000 box-processing hours per year at 500-machine scale
  6. Collision Acceptance: 10-box deliveries 2× per year (Day 120, 300) accepted as reasonable trade-off versus complexity of same-day splits
  7. Weight Law Enforcement: 20-40 lb standard protects single-person handling safety while optimizing freight tiers
  8. 11-Bin Storage System: Syrup containment + future 8-jug capacity built into site hardware from day one
  9. Flexible Scaling: BOX A can split to A1+A2 when consumption validates, BOX D lid quantity adjustable based on testing data

90-Day Shipping Cycle: Strategic Rationale

Why Cups Ship Separately from Ingredients

The 60/90 Split Decision Logic

Original Problem (v96 Model):

  • Universal 60-day cycle shipped 9 boxes per delivery (6 ingredients + 3 cups)
  • Landlord perception: 9 boxes = "major shipment event"
  • Building managers flagged large deliveries as disruptive
  • Contract renewal risk identified at 3 pilot sites

v100 Solution:

  • Separate consumables (60-day fast cycle) from durable goods (90-day slow cycle)
  • Typical delivery: 6 boxes (professional, manageable perception)
  • Cups extend to 90 days (2,000 cups = 103.7% of 90-day need)
  • Offset by +30 days → collision only 2× per year (acceptable trade-off)

Strategic Benefits:

  1. Landlord Relationship Optimization: 6-box deliveries 80% of time maintain professional appearance
  2. Hub Efficiency: Cups bypass 5-Gate QC (8,000 boxes/year skip processing at 500-machine scale)
  3. Cost Optimization: Longer cup cycle reduces annual deliveries (4× vs. 6×)
  4. Operational Flexibility: Can adjust cup frequency independently without disrupting ingredient supply
